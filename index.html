The Real Problem

Enrollment
is not
activation.

"We're enrolled in ISV Accelerate but we don't have a co-sell motion anyone is actually using."You did the right things. You met the ACE thresholds, completed the co-sell learning module, and got the badge. But without a field-aligned story, a prioritized account list, and a clear way to bring AWS sellers into live deals, the program sits on paper. Enrollment is not activation. The gap between the two is where most ISV Accelerate partnerships stall, and where TierAxis works.

"We listed on Marketplace two quarters ago and the only thing that's transacted is our patience."A listing is an address. Revenue requires a route. Most teams publish the offer, send an announcement, and wait for the field to find it. Revenue requires a route: a private offer strategy, co-sell signals tied to active ACE opportunities, and the field-facing materials that make it easy for AWS account teams to attach you to deals already in motion.

"We keep hearing about MAP and MDF but nobody has explained what we actually qualify for or when to ask."The Migration Acceleration Program, Marketing Development Funds, and ISV Accelerate co-sell benefits each have specific prerequisites: documented milestones, program enrollment gates, partner revenue thresholds, and most partners either apply too early, too late, or without the structure that makes approval move. The money is real. The access is earned, not given.
